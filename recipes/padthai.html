Padthai

this is Padthai, one of the most delicious Thai Food you can eat.

The Sauce

  • 3 tbsp Tamarind paste (concentrate)
  • 3 tbsp Palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 2 tbsp Fish sauce (use soy sauce for a vegetarian version)
  • 1 tsp Chili flakes (adjust to your heat preference)

The Stir-Fry

  • 4 oz (125g) Dried rice sticks (soaked in lukewarm water for 30 mins)
  • 2 tbsp Neutral oil
  • 1/2 cup Shrimp, tofu, or chicken
  • 2 Large eggs
  • 1/4 cup Firm tofu, cut into small batons
  • 2 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup Fresh bean sprouts
  • 3 stalks Garlic chives (or green onions), cut into 2-inch pieces

For Serving

  • Crushed roasted peanuts
  • Fresh lime wedges
  • Extra bean sprouts and cilantro

Step to makes

  1. Prep the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the tamarind paste, sugar, fish sauce, and chili flakes until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add your protein (shrimp/chicken) and firm tofu. Cook until golden, then stir in the garlic.
  3. Noodles & Sauce: Add the soaked (and drained) noodles to the pan. Pour the sauce over them. Toss constantly for 2–3 minutes until the noodles absorb the liquid and soften.
  4. The Scramble: Push everything to one side of the pan. Crack the eggs into the empty space, scramble them quickly, and then fold them back into the noodles.
  5. The Finish: Throw in the bean sprouts and garlic chives. Toss for 30 seconds—just long enough for the sprouts to wilt slightly but stay crunchy.
  6. Garnish: Plate immediately and top with a generous amount of crushed peanuts and a squeeze of fresh lime.

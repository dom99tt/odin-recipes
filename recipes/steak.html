Steak

Classic Delicious Steak that most people love

Ingredients

The Cut

  • 1 (1.5-inch thick) Steak (Ribeye, New York Strip, or Filet Mignon)
  • To taste Kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper

The Aromatics (For Basting)

  • 3 tbsp Unsalted butter
  • 3 cloves Garlic, smashed but skin on
  • 3 sprigs Fresh thyme or rosemary

For the Pan

  • 2 tbsp High-smoke point oil (Grapeseed, Avocado, or Canola oil)

Step to Make

  1. Tempering & Seasoning: Take the steak out of the fridge 30 minutes before cooking to reach room temperature. Pat it bone-dry with paper towels (moisture is the enemy of a good crust). Season aggressively with salt and pepper on all sides.
  2. The Sear: Heat a heavy cast-iron skillet over high heat until it begins to smoke. Add the oil, then carefully lay the steak in the pan away from you. Sear for 2–3 minutes without moving it until a dark brown crust forms.
  3. The Flip: Flip the steak and sear the other side for another 2 minutes.
  4. Butter Basting: Reduce heat to medium. Add the butter, garlic, and herbs to the pan. Once the butter melts and foams, tilt the pan and use a large spoon to continuously pour the hot, flavored butter over the steak for 2–3 minutes.
  5. Check Temperature: Use an instant-read thermometer to reach your desired doneness:
    • Rare: 125°F (52°C)
    • Medium-Rare: 135°F (57°C)
    • Medium: 145°F (63°C)
  6. The Rest: Transfer the steak to a warm plate or cutting board. Pour the remaining pan juices over it and let it rest for at least 5–10 minutes. This allows the fibers to relax and the juices to redistribute.

Ingredients

The Broth (Choose Your Base)

  • 2 Liters Chicken or pork bone broth
  • 4 slices Ginger
  • 3 cloves Garlic, smashed
  • 2 stalks Green onion, cut into segments
  • Optional: Goji berries, dried red dates, or a store-bought Mala (spicy Sichuan) paste.

The Dippers (The Main Event)

  • Thinly sliced meats: Ribeye beef, pork belly, or lamb shoulder.
  • Seafood: Fresh shrimp, fish balls, or squid.
  • Vegetables: Napa cabbage, bok choy, spinach, and enoki mushrooms.
  • Tofu & Noodles: Firm tofu, fried tofu puffs, and glass noodles or udon.

The Dipping Sauce (To Mix & Match)

  • Sesame paste or peanut butter
  • Soy sauce, black vinegar, and sesame oil
  • Minced garlic, cilantro, and chopped chili

Steps to Make It

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a large pot (or a dedicated electric hot pot), simmer your broth with ginger, garlic, and green onions for 15–20 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  2. Prep the Ingredients: Wash and chop all vegetables. Arrange the meats and seafood on large platters. Ensure meats are sliced paper-thin so they cook in seconds.
  3. Set the Table: Place the portable burner or electric pot in the center of the table. Surround it with the platters of raw ingredients and individual bowls for the dipping sauce.
  4. The DIY Sauce: Set out small bowls of sauce ingredients so every guest can mix their own personalized dipping blend.
  5. Let’s Eat: Bring the broth to a rolling boil. Use chopsticks or small strainers to dip your chosen ingredients into the soup
    • Meats: 20–30 seconds.
    • Vegetables: 1–2 minutes.
    • Fish balls: Until they float.
  6. The Finale: Once the ingredients are mostly finished, add the noodles to the now-extra-flavorful broth for a final comforting bowl of soup.
